How Companies Ruined Software

AI, subscriptions, "free-to-use" software, software in general, it's all gone down the drain, and companies are the primary blame of it all.

Note: This post is just a matter of opinion, and is not to be taken as gosepl.

AI Replacing Human Work

Generative AI being used to replace humans is how, to put it simply, "This is Why We Can't Have Nice Things." I can't imagine a day where the decades of hard work that humans have put in just gets replaced by something that hallucinates the majority of its output.

Generative AI plagiarizes off of the work of humans, and companies like Meta, X, Google, and Microsoft pirated thousands of books, code, interviews, audiobooks without any form of compensation. It's just sick to me to think that companies work their hardest to prevent us from pirating their stuff, but do it to our own work and think they can get away with it.

Subscriptions and "free-to-use" Software

I hate the direction we've gone with software. From being able to pay once and use forever, to having to pay an exorbitant amount of money each month to access content or work, and having your data being collected to be sold off to advertisers. Apps like Spotify, Adobe Creative Cloud, Microsoft Office, etc. are just a couple examples of stuff a lot of us use everyday, and either pay an unreasonable amount of money every month to access it, or "willingly" allow the companies to gather our usage data to sell.

It's just tiring. I've never wanted in my life to be forced to pay over $100 just to watch four or five shows that can randomly get removed and then have to get yet another one to watch it.

Physical Media and Ownership

Over the course of me realizing the course we're going towards, I've been backing up my video games through platforms like GOG or getting the physical disks of them, and going to local stores like Walmart's DVD/CD bins and local thrift stores for physical disks of my favorite movies.

It's been relieving knowing that companies can't randomly revoke my access to my movies or video games, but I can't believe that we all just normalized all of this. We truly traded our privacy and wallets for convenience. It's just only going to get worse from here if companies have their ways with our political systems.
